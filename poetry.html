Locking Antlers

November 29, 2025

... And then we were stuck,
of our own feeble accord,
with half the dance left to go.

Ten petals you did pluck,
by the blade of your sword,
in a single swift blow.

If we let the heart run amok,
to which we can afford,
will our antlers then glow?

I hope you let me, hope so.



One Cigarette

October 9, 2025

Stillness still shivers.

Hollow poles grasp at my mittens.
Fur lined boots from a friend.
Cracks in the leather stretch the little dry left.
Two layers of cotton and twill drag circles on my chest.
Three pockets of nothing hold the past's best.
The cage shutters.
Demanding a foreword, we look back;
Back at our home,
back at the cage,
back at my mittens.
Like a bag of pretzels or Swedish Fish,
it was a wise man's bliss.
You breach the bag,
its contents horrified and still still.
Hollow poles hold hollow flags.
Those boots are thankful;
Thankful for the wet and the dry,
thankful for the fur and the cracks,
thankful for the rope and the slack,
but stillness still shivers.

No longer looking, now we're staring.
Staring through sand and grass.
The ghosts of tree's children wave at the mast like the lighthouse's flash.
You look out but not away.
As the turning of the wheel matches movement with a greener place.
Green like your coat,
green like the wind,
green like the sky,
to pilot the wings you wish you could fly,
but stillness still shivers.

As gray matter crashes through columns of salt and what few distant dust remains,
you finally look away.
Now clenching the pole back,
embracing its polymer with precaution,
once you were The Watcher and now it is you who turns the faucet by the handle carved from God's chisel.
Chisel the stone from the Earth,
chisel the wings from birth,
chisel your green coat and your boots and your mittens from the walls that kept you warm and safe and still,
because stillness still shivers.

Tear the paper on the wall and scribble something inside.
It could be your name or your address.
Throw a plate at the wall,
please by all means, make a mess.
"What is it that you see or that you saw?
For you are The Watcher,
maker of green and gray.
You thought you could make a house out of clay but all I see are hills and hills and hills.
It is you who watches them form and who knows.
Knows of the sea foam,
knows of its white lie,
knows of its orange analog filter."
The pole sinks in from the heat like a curtsy to the core,
when stillness still shivers.

A rip in your mittens in between the fingers.
It's time to turn in but the pole begs.
It begs and it begs and it begs.
It pleads, it screams, it says,
"Do you not feel the turbulence?
Does the shake not bind your soul like the blinds to the darkness or icing to the cake?
Do you not feel the green or the gray?
Do you not quiver under cold steel words?
Do you not pierce the water with lead?
Do you not hold the foam to your lips and ask for a kiss as it traces lines on your back?
Do you not hold the curve?
Do you not influence the rudder in its rudeness?
You are not The Watcher!
You are a trick and a cheap!
A master of deception,
piecing fragments of frills in boxes every afternoon after.
You see not the grass that is green nor the smoke that is gray.
You see red and brown.
Blood underneath your nails and mud between your fingers.
You are stuck on an overhang,
spinning in place.
Your stamina, macerated by deceit and masqueraded as hope,
reflected by a shield of mirrors from the sands of somewhere.
You could have been statues.
You could have been books.
You could have been a drum for peace."

"And yet, stillness still shivers," you replied.
"I was close.
I was close as any two things could be.
Like the hair to my face.
Like the tire to the road.
Like a pin to a sash.
Like a branch to its flower.
Like an animal to its cage.
And for what?
I had sauntered along the path of least resistance for a wisdom tooth's time,
and had not but parted the view from my window.
I made the tea and the tools to take them far,
but they had not the coat.
Stuck in the wash and buried under layers of sediment.
You could never understand the mittens nor the boots.
I felt the tremblings, as did you.
I felt them everyday, even through the boots and through my shield.
I felt the green and the gray.
I've felt all that I needed to feel.
If I find what I lack is the red and the brown then I will take them with me too.
I felt the foam,
God yes have I felt the foam.
And I have felt the steel words.
I have felt the bones and their marrow,
the blinds and the shudder,
the books and the statues,
the drum and the siren that calls,
and I would burn it.
That's right I would burn it.
I will take it with me and burn it.
I'm not joking, I will burn it, I really will burn it,
because you were right about one thing.
I am not The Watcher.
I am a liar and thief.
A beggar and chooser,
a captain and chief of my only desire,
who let it slip away under the cruel sheets of paper that leave cuts on my feet.
I walked for miles.
I really did, and I think I would do it again if it meant I could be on this boat again.
I leave only now because I know I don't need to come back to return.
I have all now.
I have the land and the sea,
and all the distance in between.
I have the stand and the plea,
and every dollar spent in spree.
I am made of the grass and the metal,
the gray and the green.
I cry for you and the echoes,
unwilling but able.
I cry for the signs and the geckos,
unrelenting yet stable.
I cry for pain and for truth,
a stain on my youth.
I cry for claims and the proof,
barred from entering a truce.
I cry because I can,
because there's nothing I can do,
because there's nothing left of you.
Yes, stillness still shivers,
but you don't have to."
